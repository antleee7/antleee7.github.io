I am interested in the intersection of homogeneous dynamics and Lie theory, with motivations coming from number theory and hyperbolic geometry. Much of my recent work is inspired by Selberg's Prime Geodesic Theorem and related counting problems for rank 1 Riemannian symmetric spaces.

Concretely, I've worked on questions involving arithmetic subgroups of $\operatorname{PSL}_2(\mathbb{C})$, and I am currently interested in extending these ideas to arithmetic subgroups of $\operatorname{SO}^+(n,1)$ for $n > 3$. More broadly, I am interested in understanding such counting problems for arithmetic subgroups of Lie groups over local fields, including through volume formulas due to G. Prasad. My interest in this direction is building on a strong background in algebraic geometry.
